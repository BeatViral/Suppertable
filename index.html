The unlock

Airbnb unlocked the spare room. SupperTable unlocks the home kitchen.

The product is not just a booking page. The real company is a trust engine: verified hosts, menu transparency, food-safety training, guest rules, payments, reviews, incident reporting, and council-aware operating records.

Product flow

One simple marketplace, three intelligent paths.

Guests see appetite and trust. Hosts see a guided income path. Admins see the compliance picture that makes the whole model serious.

Guest app

Find a table that feels personal, verified, and delicious.

  1. Browse approved listings by cuisine, date, price, dietary needs, and suburb.
  2. Open a meal page with photos, host story, set menu, allergens, seats, and house rules.
  3. Book prepaid seats, complete guest verification, and receive the exact address after confirmation.
  4. Arrive for a small on-premise meal, then review the host and dining experience.

Guest experience

Make it feel easier than a restaurant booking, but warmer than delivery.

The guest should never feel like they are taking a risk on a random house. They should feel like they found a rare table that has been thoughtfully prepared, verified, and protected.

  • Photos of the meal and dining space before booking
  • Clear menu, ingredients, allergens, and dietary notes
  • Guest identity verification and behaviour rules
  • Payment before arrival with address released after confirmation

Trust architecture

The compliance layer is the moat.

SupperTable should not claim every home is automatically approved. It should show a safety-supported pathway: local requirements, menu risk, host standards, guest conduct, insurance status, and auditability.

Verification Host ID, guest ID, reviews, admin status
Food safety Training, allergens, temperature guidance, cleaning rules
Local pathway Notification, application, permit, approval, or inspection prompts
Risk control Menu tiering, pause rules, incidents, exportable logs
