The unlock

Airbnb unlocked the spare room. SupperTable unlocks the home kitchen, without inviting strangers inside.

The product is not just an ordering page. The real company is a trust engine: verified hosts, menu transparency, food-safety training, customer verification, payments, reviews, incident reporting, pickup/delivery controls, and council-aware operating records.

Product flow

One simple marketplace, three intelligent paths.

Customers see appetite and trust. Hosts see a guided income path. Admins see the compliance picture that makes the whole model serious.

Customer app

Find a home kitchen that feels personal, verified, and delicious.

  1. Browse verified home kitchens by cuisine, pickup window, delivery radius, price, dietary needs, and suburb.
  2. Open a meal page with photos, host story, set menu, allergens, portion count, packaging, and fulfilment notes.
  3. Place a prepaid pickup or delivery order, complete account verification, and receive order updates after confirmation.
  4. Enjoy a home-cooked meal, then review the host, food accuracy, packaging, pickup, and delivery experience.

Customer experience

Make it feel as easy as delivery, but flexible enough for pickup.

The customer should never feel like they are taking a risk on a random kitchen. They should feel like they found a real local cook whose food is thoughtfully prepared, verified, packaged, and protected.

  • Photos of the meal, kitchen standard, and packaging before ordering
  • Clear menu, ingredients, allergens, and dietary notes
  • Customer identity, payment, pickup, and delivery protections
  • Prepaid order with pickup/delivery window, handoff notes, and support trail

Trust architecture

The compliance layer is the moat.

Hosts go live only after completing SupperTable's verification and local compliance pathway, with menu risk, host standards, pickup/delivery controls, insurance status, and auditability built into the model.

Verification Host ID, customer ID, reviews, admin status
Food safety Training, allergens, temperature guidance, cleaning rules
Local pathway Notification, application, permit, approval, inspection, pickup, or delivery prompts
Risk control Menu tiering, packaging rules, pause rules, incidents, exportable logs
